Responsive Redesign

The goal of this assignment is for you to practice the workflow of redesigning a simple website. You should take away the skills necessary to analyze and identify flaws in an existing interface, create low-fidelity and high-fidelity prototypes for various screen sizes, and build a responsive website based on those prototypes.

I chose the Books on the Square website because I love visiting this store, but I don't think that the current site shows off the positive traits that the bookstore has to offer.



Old Site - Usability Issues

way too much information present

There is a toon of information that BOTS is trying to share, but the way that the website uses hierarchy makes it hard to access any particular task. All of the possible services that the website has to offer are in the menu bar

Bad Color Scheme, Lack of Contrast

The current Books on the Square site uses too many different colors, and because of the grey background, many of the colors don't show with enough contrast and make it hard to navigate the website. Additionally, some of the links are missing images and many images are missing their alt text. This is a big problem when it comes to making websites accessible.

Hard to Search for Books

As a bookstore, I think that BOTS prioritizes its events and newsletter features (among others) over its book searching feature. In the website redesign, I decided to make it easier to look through books and complete the online ordering process that exists but is hard to locate on the old website. I put the website through the webAIM WAVE software, which revealed a ton of different problems with the site related to contrast, redundant links, alt text, and general placement of divs.

Books on the Square Redesign

Here is my attempt at redesigning the BOTS home page. I worked to make the page more responsive, especially in the nav bar. I did this mainly using media queries, and created html components to enable horizontal scrolling. The page has buttons for alternate pages, but I didn't make any since the redesign is a proof of concept.
